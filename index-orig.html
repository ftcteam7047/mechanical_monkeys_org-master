Who We Are

Let us introduce ourselves.

FTC Team

First Tech Challenge (FTC) is one of the levels from the FIRST robotics program. Students design, build, program, fundraise, and drive robots for competitions. Teams then compete head on in tournaments that advance all the way to a global final. We are proud to be an FTC Team.

Middle Schoolers

All our members are middle schoolers at Bloomfield Hills Middle School. We have 6 eighth graders, 2 seventh graders, and 1 sixth grader. In all other states, FTC is for high schoolers only. However, in the state of Michigan FTC is for middle schoolers.

Learners

Through the FTC program, we get a hands-on experience with programming, building, designing, teamwork, and so much more. This is a unique opportunity specific to STEM (science, technology, engineering, math) which helps foster youth to have a better understanding of STEM jobs, with the intent to help them go into this ever-expanding field.

Our Journey

From the drawing board to the competition.

  • September 9

    The Kickoff

    Our team participated at the first Bloomfield Hills High School FTC Kickoff. The day before, many of our team members helped set-up the event. Here, we took classes and demonstrated our robot to other teams. As a team, we watched this year's game challenge video- Relic Recovery. Immediately, we started strategizing and discussing the game.

  • September 9- October 7

    Designing and Building

    Right after the kickoff, we got together and strategized. In the meetings that followed, we continued to strategize and then design. Everyone contributed ideas which led to a prototyping session. Finally, we started to build our robot with input from our mechanical mentor, George. All builders were assigned a different part of the robot to build.

    The Mason Qualifier was the competition we attended last year where we took home the Inspire Award, the most prestigous award in FTC. This year, we will be returning there to compete again. We hope to have similar success.

The Programming Team

Michael, Om, Bowie

In FTC, teams need to program their robot so they can control it during the driver-control period, and have the robot perform various tasks with pre-programmed instructions. Students who choose to program use Java. Our programming team consists of two veteran FTC programmers. This year, our programming team plans to use Google Brain's TensorFlow so they can use machine learning to identify the game's "jewels". Tensorflow will be able to help the robot identify the "jewel" and the colors of the "jewel". Using this, the robot will be able to figure out which "jewel" to remove.

Our Outreach

FTC Camp

An important part of FIRST's mission is to spread STEM. That's why all FTC teams do outreach, teaching the community about STEM. In the past, our outreach included talking about FIRST on the radio, showing off our robot at a Metroparent Event, and helping out a FLL Jr. Event. This season, two of our team mates, Heather and Michael, were counselors at a district FTC Camp where they taught classes to FTC rookies and each lead a team. They acted as coaches and mentors for these teams, leading them through a mock FTC season compiled into 5 days. At the competition at the end of the camp, Michael's team won first in the robot game with Heather's team close behind with second place. Heather's team also won the award for best judging presentation. The Mechanical Monkeys plan to do more outreach this year, so check back soon for updates.
